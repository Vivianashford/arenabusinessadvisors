Business Valuation Methods for Service Companies

By Jean Louis Hardy | March 23, 2026 | 8 min read

If you own a service business and you have ever wondered what it is actually worth, the honest answer is: it depends entirely on how a buyer would run it without you.

That single factor - transferability - creates valuation swings of 2-4x on the same underlying financials. Two plumbing companies with identical $2M EBITDA can be valued at $6M and $14M respectively.

The Three Valuation Methods

1. EBITDA Multiple Method

The standard for service businesses doing $3M+ in revenue. Typical multiples for service businesses ($5M-$50M revenue): Owner-dependent, project-based: 2.5-3.5x. Moderate systems, some recurring: 3.5-5x. Team-managed, recurring revenue: 5-7x. Scalable platform, strong growth: 7-10x.

2. Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE)

Used for smaller service businesses under $3M revenue. SDE adds back owner compensation, personal expenses, and one-time costs. Typical multiples: 1.5-3.5x SDE.

3. Revenue-Based Valuation

Sometimes used for high-growth service businesses or those with strong recurring revenue. MSPs and SaaS-adjacent services may trade at 1-3x revenue.

The Adjustments That Matter

Buyers normalize your EBITDA by adjusting for: owner compensation, personal expenses, one-time costs, below-market rent, and related party transactions. These adjustments can swing your effective EBITDA by 20-40%.

What Buyers Actually Calculate

Sophisticated buyers run a risk-adjusted model with discounts: owner dependency (-15% to -40%), customer concentration (-10% to -30%), no documented processes (-10% to -20%), key employee risk (-5% to -15%), declining revenue trend (-10% to -25%). These discounts stack.
