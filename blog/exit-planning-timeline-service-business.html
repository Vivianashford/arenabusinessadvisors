The Exit Planning Timeline: When to Start Preparing Your Service Business for Sale

By Jean Louis Hardy | March 23, 2026 | 7 min read

The biggest mistake service business owners make about exit planning is timing. They start thinking about selling when they are ready to leave. By then, it is 2-3 years too late to maximize value.

The preparation window for a premium service business exit is 24-36 months.

The Four-Phase Timeline

Phase 1: Align (Months 1-3)

Assessment and foundation. Get a baseline valuation. Identify every gap. Build the operating system: weekly meetings, KPI dashboards, accountability structure.

Phase 2: Map (Months 4-9)

Documentation and systematization. Write SOPs for every critical process. Build training programs. Develop 2-3 leaders. Begin transitioning client relationships. Clean up financials.

Phase 3: Automate (Months 10-18)

Prove the system works. Owner takes extended time away. Management team handles all decisions. Revenue maintains or grows during owner absence.

Phase 4: Scale (Months 19-30)

Go to market from a position of strength. Engage M&A advisor. Prepare CIM. Identify buyers. Manage LOI, diligence, and closing.

What Happens If You Start Too Late?

Owners who go to market within 6 months face: lower multiples, longer earnouts (2-3 years vs 12 months), more seller financing, higher deal failure rates, and less negotiating leverage.

The irony: owners who start early often find the last 2 years of ownership the most satisfying period of their career.
