What Private Equity Looks for When Buying Service Businesses

By Jean Louis Hardy | March 23, 2026 | 9 min read

Private equity firms have acquired more service businesses in the last 5 years than in the previous 20 combined. PE-backed platforms in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, IT services, staffing, dental, and dozens of other verticals are actively buying.

The PE Acquisition Thesis

PE firms buy a "platform" company at 5-7x EBITDA, bolt on 5-15 smaller acquisitions at 3-5x, professionalize management, then sell the combined entity at 8-12x. Result: 3-5x return in 5-7 years.

The 8 Things PE Evaluates

1. EBITDA Quality: Minimum $1.5M-$2M adjusted EBITDA. 3 years consistent or growing.

2. Revenue Composition: 60%+ recurring or contract-based revenue preferred.

3. Customer Concentration: No single customer over 10-15% of revenue.

4. Management Team: Strong #2 (COO/GM), capable sales leader, reliable ops manager.

5. Scalability: Can the model be replicated in new geographies?

6. Industry Tailwinds: Fragmentation, essential services, aging owner demographics.

7. Technology: Modern CRM, project management, financial reporting.

8. Geographic Density: Strong market position in specific metros.

How to Position as a Platform Acquisition

Build a management team. Document every process. Diversify customers below 15%. Convert to recurring revenue. Invest in software. Clean your financials. Build a clear growth story.
